DIFFERENT PRIORITIES IN THE SINHALA AND TAMIL PRESS

Tamil Headlines

Perera stated that the government has an agreement with plantation companies and called for audits of profit and loss accounts to ensure plantation workers receive the maximum share of income. A temporary suspension has been imposed on constructing a Buddhist place of worship. Doctors to begin trade union action from today (17th).

Thinakaran: A decision has been made to establish drug prevention committees in government, private, and educational institutions. An invitation letter has been sent to Ranil Wickremesinghe; a CID team has travelled to England to investigate. The poppy flower was pinned on the prime minister.

Thirumavalavan also noted that military personnel from many countries are studying LTTE leader Prabhakaran's war strategies. Gajendrakumar Ponnambalam warned that the Tamil Nadu government's silence poses a danger to Eelam Tamils. The country's drug supply is almost depleted, with police warning of severe shortages due to blocked shipments.

Thamilan: Services provided by government doctors are restricted, putting hospital operations at risk of paralysis today (17th). Governor of the Southern Province Bandula Harischandra has passed away.

TAMIL PRESS PRIORITIES
Photo credits: The Island

"Even after sending three letters to the President, no reply has been received so far,” states Shritharan MP in parliament

Thinakkural, Virakesari

ITAK MP S. Shritharan stated that, despite sending three letters to the President requesting the release of lands including the Maaveerar Thuyilum Illam (cemeteries) in Theravil and the Kilinochchi Maha Vidyalaya, as well as calling for an end to military interference in civil activities, he has not received a response. Speaking in parliament, Shritharan emphasised that soldiers desecrating these sites by walking on graves with boots widens the gap in reconciliation instead of fostering unity. He pointed out that, just as the current government—which also has a history of struggle—recently commemorated Sinhala youth killed during the insurrection, they must understand the similar pain and sentiments of the Tamil people regarding their own dead. Consequently, he urged the government to release these burial grounds in time for Maaveerar Naal, noting that Minister Chandrashekhar had previously indicated the President's willingness to release such lands in the North and East.

Attempt to construct a Buddhist place of worship temporarily suspended

Virakesari

Illegal construction work led by a monk was reportedly undertaken yesterday (16th) without authorisation at the Sri Sambuddha Jayanthi Bodhi Raja Vihara premises near the Dutch Bay coast at Fort Frederick, Trincomalee. Officials from the Department of Coast Conservation and Coastal Resource Management and journalists who visited to inspect the unauthorised activities were threatened and forced to leave the site. Following a complaint lodged by department officials at the Trincomalee Harbour Police, construction work has now been temporarily suspended, and the police have filed a court case regarding the incident. Reports indicate that, despite a previous attempt earlier this month to remove an illegal canteen and an ongoing legal dispute with the municipal council, permanent construction was secretly initiated overnight on Saturday (15th) without the required approval from the relevant authorities.

“The government has a deal with the plantation companies. Should take action to audit the profit and loss and provide the maximum benefit of the income to the plantation workers" - Ajith P Perera

Thamilan, Thinakkural

SJB MP Ajith P. Perera alleged that the government has an agreement with plantation companies and called on the authorities to audit these companies' profits to ensure workers receive the maximum benefit. Perera claimed that the government had misrepresented a statement by SJB MP Rohini Kavirathna on the Rs. 200 wage increase for political purposes, clarifying that she had merely highlighted legal procedural flaws. He further alleged that plantation owners supported President Anura Kumara Dissanayake's victory and informally refer to him as 'Machan.' Perera also criticised the 2026 Budget, claiming it favours the capitalist class while offering no relief to the middle class.

"Prabhakaran is an unbeatable leader!” - Thirumavalavan praises in Jaffna

Thamilan, Thinakkural

Viduthalai Chiruthaigal Katchi (VCK) Leader Thol. Thirumavalavan stated that countries worldwide study LTTE leader Velupillai Prabhakaran's military strategies in their military colleges due to his tactical skill and reputation for invincibility. Speaking at an event at Nallur Kittu Park in Jaffna, Thirumavalavan recalled his close association with the LTTE leader and praised Prabhakaran's geopolitical insight, referencing his 2002 description of Norway as the friendly face of America. He commented that, unlike current leaders who speak frequently, the entire world would wait for Prabhakaran's single annual address on Maaveerar Naal (Great Heroes' Day). He further observed that Prabhakaran never portrayed the Indian government as an enemy, understanding that Tamil aspirations could only be met with India's support. Thinakkural also quoted Thirumavalavan as saying that Eelam Tamils should abandon the belief that Tamil Eelam could be achieved simply through agitation in Tamil Nadu. Instead, he urged unity under a single leadership to address the Tamil issue.

SINHALA PRESS PRIORITIES
Photo credits: Ada Derana

“Money has even been taken from the President’s Fund to frame Shiranthi Rajapaksa’s photograph” – Minister of Justice Harshana Nanayakkara

Lankadeepa, Dinamina

Minister of Justice and National Integration, Attorney-at-Law Harshana Nanayakkara, stated in parliament on Saturday (15th) that even the Rs. 9,270 spent to laminate and frame a photograph of former First Lady Shiranthi Rajapaksa required for her official visit to China was paid from the President’s Fund. He added that Rs. 660,000 was spent on Pirith chanting at the Carlton Residence in Tangalle on April 9, 2014, marking Namal Rajapaksa’s birthday and New Year celebrations. The Minister made this statement during the Committee Stage debate on the Appropriation Bill. Nanayakkara stated that this highlights how former presidents utilised their expenditure heads while state revenue was negative.

Trawler and five individuals who brought drugs to Kirinda seized

Divaina, Mawbima, Dinamina

A multi-day fishing trawler named Kasun Putha from Gandara, allegedly transporting 329 kg of ice (crystal methamphetamine) to the Kirinda, Adagalawella coast, was seized at sea by officers of the Sri Lanka Navy (SLN) and Police Narcotics Bureau (PNB). The trawler was then brought to Tangalle Fisheries Harbour on Saturday (15th). The five suspects aboard were produced before the Tissamaharama court yesterday (16th) by the Narcotics Special Investigation Unit. The Tissamaharama Magistrate ordered the suspects to be detained and questioned until November 21.

“Mahinda, Namal, Chandima, Seetha, Bandula, Lanza, Jagath Kumara and Lokuge have received houses at Viyathpura, then given to outsiders” – Wasantha Samarasinghe

Dinamina

Minister of Trade, Commerce, Food Security and Cooperative Development Wasantha Samarasinghe stated in parliament on Saturday (15th) that Rs. 529 million has been spent recently to provide Viyathpura houses to MPs and ministers. He added that Mahinda Rajapaksa, Namal Rajapaksa, Chandima Weerakkody, Seetha Arambepola, Bandula Gunawardana, Nimal Lanza, Jagath Kumara, and Gamini Lokuge also received houses in this scheme, and subsequently these houses were given to outsiders. Samarasinghe made these remarks during the Committee Stage debate of the 2026 Appropriation Bill.

“ITAK to hold discussions with the President next week” – Shanakyan

Dinamina

Illankai Tamil Arasu Kadchi (ITAK) Batticaloa District MP Shanakiyan Rasamanickam announced in parliament that a meeting between President Anura Kumara Dissanayake and ITAK is scheduled for next week. Rasamanickam made this statement during the Second Reading of the Budget on Friday (14th). Further commenting, Rasamanickam stated, "We are meeting in this way to discuss the problems faced by the Tamil people. Our party has placed its trust in the president."

Issuing prescriptions for medicines unavailable in state hospitals but available externally to be halted; recommendations for external tests also to be stopped – firm decision from the GMOA

Lankadeepa, Mawbima, Aruna, Virakesari, Thinakkural, Thamilan

The Government Medical Officers’ Association (GMOA) announced that doctors in all hospitals nationwide will restrict certain services from today (17th). The GMOA is implementing this as a form of trade union action to protest the government’s failure to address their demands in the recent budget. Measures include stopping the provision of prescriptions for medicines not available in state hospitals but available in outside pharmacies and restricting recommendations for tests by external laboratories.

CID travels to London, bypassing AG, to verify invitation received by Ranil Wickremesinghe from university

Lankadeepa, Divaina, Mawbima, Thinakaran, Thinakkural

A five-member Criminal Investigation Department (CID) team has travelled to the United Kingdom (UK) to investigate the authenticity of the invitation letter reportedly sent by the University of Wolverhampton to former President Ranil Wickremesinghe. However, news reports state that this visit was carried out without informing the Attorney General (AG). Furthermore, it is reported that the AG’s Department was not notified about the CID team's trip, nor were instructions sought from senior CID officers leading the investigation prior to their departure. The team, led by a Deputy Inspector General (DIG) and including an Assistant Superintendent of Police (ASP), a Chief Inspector (CI), and two junior officers, flew to the UK earlier this week for a five-day visit.

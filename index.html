"The Tamil people despise the Ministry of Defence, which is responsible for all their suffering" – Gajendrakumar Ponnambalam

Thinakkural, Virakesari, Thamilan

All Ceylon Tamil Congress (ACTC) MP Gajendrakumar Ponnambalam asserted that Tamil people greatly despise the Ministry of Defence, which he claimed is responsible for all suffering endured by Tamils during and after the civil war. Speaking in Parliament yesterday (18th), he alleged that military personnel in the North and East are involved in drug distribution, noting that the drug problem escalated after the war, unlike under the LTTE administration. Ponnambalam demanded that the government acknowledge that war crimes and human rights violations took place during the war, citing evidence provided by credible members within the military. He insisted that because the government and military face such accusations, they must permit international investigations to earn the trust of the Tamil people.

"Will Tamils ever get justice in Sri Lanka? The true face of the Anura government exposed today; Buddhist monks act as the country’s executive presidents" – Jaffna University Students' Union strongly condemns

Virakesari, Thinakkural

Representatives of the Jaffna University Students’ Union asserted that Sinhala Buddhist majoritarianism is at the root of the ethnic problem and the war, leading to continued Buddhist-Sinhala dominance over Tamil lands in the name of Buddha worship. At a media briefing yesterday (18th), they stated that Tamil people who voted in hope of a non-racist political culture have been utterly betrayed by the government through the illegal reinstallation of the statue in Trincomalee. The Union questioned why the law is not enforced against those who illegally installed the statue, disrupted public order, and assaulted the police. They warned that they would not accept the systematic land grabs carried out under the guise of worship, concluding that the state machinery is entirely subservient to the monks.

"Does placing Buddha statues on land belonging to Lord Shiva foster reconciliation?" – Premachandran questions

Virakesari

Eelam People’s Revolutionary Liberation Front (EPRLF) leader and DTNA Spokesperson Suresh Premachandran stated that the illegal construction of Buddha statues and acts of Buddhisation in Trincomalee and around Koneswaram Temple undermine its historical significance and the religious sentiments of Tamils, and will never contribute to Sri Lanka’s development or ethnic unity. He noted that successive Sinhala governments have surrounded the base of the Koneswaram Temple, a sacred site cited in the Devaarams [Hindu devotional poems] and over 2,500 years old, with Buddha statues and Buddhist establishments. Premachandran pointed out that sites traditionally used by Tamils for final rites, such as the Kannya Hot Water Wells, have been taken over by Buddhist monks who are now administering the site and demolishing Hindu temples in the area.

"Do not release land held by the military! National security is vital to the country" – SJB MP Nalin Bandara insists in Parliament

Thinakkural, Thamilan

SJB MP Nalin Bandara urged the government not to release land in the Northern Province linked to national security, asserting that national security must take precedence over individual ownership when making decisions on land release. Speaking in Parliament yesterday (18th), he argued that the 30-year war has not completely ended, and that future conflicts could arise, making it essential for the military to retain strategically important land. Bandara accused the government of acting irresponsibly and compromising national security due to promises made to win votes in the North. He recommended that, rather than releasing security-related land, the government should provide alternative land or adequate compensation to rightful owners.

"There is no ban on commemorating relatives; if the LTTE is commemorated, legal action will be taken" – Nalinda Jayatissa

Virakesari

Cabinet Spokesperson Nalinda Jayatissa stated that there are no restrictions on citizens commemorating relatives who died during the country’s 30-year civil war, and that all citizens have the right to remembrance. However, he stressed that if terrorists are commemorated under this pretext, legal action will be taken against those involved. Minister Jayatissa further noted that since the current government took office in November last year, various groups have tried to stir up racism, but these attempts have failed. He stated that the government is dedicated to ensuring that such a situation does not recur, and will take all necessary steps to foster ethnic unity and reconciliation to maintain peace and order.

SINHALA PRESS PRIORITIES
Photo credits: Sri Lanka Mirror

NPP secures control of Seethawaka Pradeshiya Sabha six months after the election

Lankadeepa, Divaina, Mawbima, Dinamina

Six months after the Local Government (LG) Election, the NPP established control of the Seethawaka Pradeshiya Sabha yesterday (18th). Accordingly, P.K. Premarathna, a member of the NPP, was appointed Chairperson of the Seethawaka Pradeshiya Sabha. In the previous LG Election, no party or independent group secured more than 50% of seats required to form a majority in the Seethawaka Pradeshiya Sabha. The NPP won 23 seats, while the remaining 24 seats went to various opposition parties. As a result, the Seethawaka Pradeshiya Sabha, which had failed to form a majority for six months, reconvened yesterday (18th). It was first determined whether the chairperson would be elected by open or secret ballot. The 23 NPP members and one Sarvajana Balaya opposition member opted for a secret ballot. Subsequently, a vote was held and NPP’s Premarathna was elected as Chairperson of the Seethawaka Pradeshiya Sabha.

"None of the government bungalows have been given to MPs or ministers" – Nalinda Jayatissa

Dinamina

Cabinet Spokesperson Minister Nalinda Jayatissa stated that no government bungalows or houses have been allocated to any MP, minister, or politician. He made this comment yesterday (18th) during the Cabinet decisions media briefing at the Department of Government Information. Jayatissa added that, out of 32 government bungalows, six have been temporarily leased: four to the Ministry of Justice, one to the Police Department, and one to the Commission to Investigate Allegations of Bribery or Corruption (CIABOC).

A government programme to resolve issues faced by local potato farmers

Dinamina

The government intends to establish a comprehensive programme with short-, medium-, and long-term plans to resolve the problems faced by local potato farmers. The programme includes proposals to purchase potatoes from farmers at Rs. 200–300 per kilogram, develop a mechanism for procurement through Sathosa, and increase the tax on imported potatoes. Discussions on the issue took place yesterday (18th) at the Presidential Secretariat, attended by Minister of Agriculture, Livestock, Land and Irrigation K.D. Lalkantha, Minister of Trade, Commerce, Food Security and Cooperative Development Wasantha Samarasinghe, and potato farmers.

"I will not speak in Parliament for a month if 20,000 people do not attend the Nugegoda rally" – MP Chamara Sampath

Lankadeepa

New Democratic Front (NDF) MP Chamara Sampath has stated that he will refrain from speaking in Parliament for one month if 20,000 people do not attend the anti-government rally scheduled for November 21. The rally is being organised by the united opposition against the government. Sampath made this statement in Parliament yesterday (18th).

Trincomalee statue incident: President requests report from Secretary to Public Security | "Will not leave any room for racism; investigations into murders such as Thajudeen’s will be conducted thoroughly in future" – President

Lankadeepa, Divaina, Mawbima, Dinamina, Aruna, Virakesari, Thinakaran, Thinakkural, Thamilan

President Anura Kumara Dissanayake stated in Parliament yesterday (18th) that he has directed the Secretary to the Ministry of Public Security to provide a report on the incident at the Sri Sambuddha Jayanthi Bodhiraja Viharaya in Trincomalee. He further stated that the drug trade and criminal gangs will be completely eradicated, and that he is dedicated to building a clean society for future generations by defeating all organised conspiracies. The president added that investigations into crimes such as the death of Thajudeen and the Easter Sunday attack will be conducted properly in the future. He emphasised that those who have been defeated aim to instigate racism, but stressed that he would not allow racism to re-emerge. The president made these remarks during the debate on the expenditure heads of the Ministry of Defence and the Ministry of Public Security and Parliamentary Affairs.

Draft approved: act to be tabled in Parliament soon — pensions of 330 former MPs cancelled

Lankadeepa, Divaina, Mawbima, Dinamina, Virakesari, Thinakaran

The Cabinet has granted approval to table the Parliamentary Pensions Law (Repeal) Bill in Parliament. According to Cabinet Spokesperson Minister Nalinda Jayatissa, after this Bill is enacted, MPs will no longer be eligible for a pension. He made this announcement yesterday (18th) at the Cabinet decisions media briefing held at the Department of Government Information.
